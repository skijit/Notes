Esther Transcription Notes
====================

## SECTION A

![esther section a transcription](/resources/docs/music/esther-sec-a.png)

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 1**

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| D | D | F#- | C | G |
| I | I | iii | bVII | IV |

- I - (IV => iv)
- Most frequent chord: I
- **Comments**:
    - 5 bars don't feel awkward: the extra 1st bar of D is like floating a little longer on the home chord.
    - The bVII is like a IV of the G, so it has a plagal cadence / suspensiony sound that works even though it's not diatonic or a secondary V
        - secondary IV's seems to work nicely (or just moving in 4ths)
    - Nice descending line of D. Db, C, B
    - The iii chord *unsettles* the I and the bVII helps take us to IV

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 2**

| 6 | 7 | 8 | 9 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| G- | D | G | G- |
| iv | I | IV | iv |

- (IV => iv) - (iv ->> I)
- Most frequent chord: iv
- **Techniques**:
    - iv / IV variation 
- **Comments**:
    - Simply plays around with the sound of the iv, and then takes us back home to I

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 3**

| 10 | 11 | 12 | 13 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| D | G | G#o | A |
| I | IV | #ivo | V |

- (iv ->> I) - (V => V7)
- **Comments**
    - Walks us to the V

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  

**Phrase 4**

| 14 | 15 | 16 | 17 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| A7 | D | A | D- |
| V7 | I | V | i |

- (V => V7) - (i)
- Most frequent chord: V
- **Techniques**:
    - resolve to parallel minor 
- **Comments**:
    - Big cadence, makes you think a solid I is coming, but it gives you i instead

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;

| Start Chord | I | iv | I | V7 |

- Most frequent chords: Tied at 4 between I and IV

## Section B

![esther section b transcription](/resources/docs/music/esther-sec-b.png)

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 1**

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| F | F+ | D/F | F7 |
| I | I+ | I6 | I7 |

- (i => (to rel maj) I) - (I7 ->> IV)
- **Techniques**:
    - Modulates to the relative major
    - Lengthening the I sound, sets up a resolution  
- **Comments**:
    - By lengthening into a I7, it kinda requires some type of resolution as the next chord (e.g. the IV)

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 2**

| 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| Bb | Bb- | F | F |
| IV | iv | I | I |

- (I7 =>> IV) - (I)
- **Techniques**:
    - IV/iv variations      
- **Comments**:
    - Checking out the IV/iv, then back home

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 3**

| 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| C | C | F | F |
| V | V | I | I |

- (V) - (I)

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  
**Phrase 4**

| 9 | 10 | 11 | 12 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| G- | G- | C | C |
| ii | ii | V | V |
| -- | -- | Last | Ending  |
| -- | -- | Bb- | Eb7 |
| -- | -- | V | V |

- (ii) - (V =>> I)
- **Techniques**:
  - Lengthens the I sound by walking up from the 5th scale degree (the major equivalent of Bond)  
- **Comments**:
    - Turnaround bar using the first ending
    - Last ending sets up a modulation to Ab
    - Up a m3: This is a legit key change
        - If we we modulated to G, we'd be hitting a lot of the same chord/roots
        - Ab has 4 of the same notes a F

## Section C

![esther section c transcription](/resources/docs/music/esther-sec-c.png)
&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  

**Phrase 1**

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| Ab | Ao | Bb- | Eb |
| I | #Io | ii | V |

- (V =>> I) - (V)

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  

**Phrase 2**

| 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| Db | Db | Eb | Eb |
| IV | IV | V | V |

- (IV) - (V)
- Last Ending: (IV) - (V -> (mod up half step) )

&nbsp;  
&nbsp;  

**Section C Synposis**
- Histogram:
    - I 1
    - #Io 1
    - ii 1
    - IV 2
    - V 3
- So this section was really about the V sound, which leads into an interesting modulation up a half-step, because we're really really expecting that I.

## Section D

![esther section d transcription](/resources/docs/music/esther-sec-d.png)

- Only 1 harmonic phrase

| 1-4 | 5-8 | 9-12 | 13- 16 | 17-20 | 
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | 
| E | E- | F#- | F | B |
| I | i | ii | II | V |

- **Comments**
    - Melodic phrases are broken up in 4 bars
    - Good for solos:
        - harmonic rhythm is slower
        - final chord is a V, so there's a nice turnaround 
    - i : switch to parallel minor
    - II : basically a V of V
